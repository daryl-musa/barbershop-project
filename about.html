Our Story

How K Steve Barbers Started

From one chair in a rented corner shop to a full barbershop on Kabarnet Road.

It started with one chair and a broken mirror

Steve Kamau opened the original shop in 2019 in a small rented space along Kabarnet Road, barely big enough for one chair and a bench for waiting customers. He'd learned to cut hair from his uncle's shop in Kayole, and figured Kabarnet Road had enough foot traffic and not enough good barbers to make it work.

The first few months were slow. Most days meant five or six customers, and Steve did every single cut himself. By early 2020 word had spread enough that he brought in Brian, a friend from barber college who specialized in beard work, just before things got difficult for small businesses everywhere. The shop survived on regulars who kept coming back even when most people stayed home.

Felix joined in 2022, bringing experience with natural hair, afros, and locs that the shop didn't have before. Today the shop runs three chairs most days, and Steve still does cuts himself most mornings before handling the business side in the afternoon.

What we won't do

We don't double-book chairs to squeeze in more customers. We don't rush a fade because someone's in a hurry. If a cut takes thirty-five minutes to get right, it takes thirty-five minutes. That's the one rule that hasn't changed since the shop had one chair.

Shop Facts

  • Opened: March 2019
  • Location: Kabarnet Road, Nairobi
  • Chairs: 3
  • Barbers: Steve, Brian, Felix
  • Average wait: 15 to 20 minutes
  • Walk-ins only, no app required

Our Approach

Every barber here trains for at least two years before working solo on a paying customer. We'd rather lose a rushed customer than gain a bad reputation.
